Mahatma Gandhi

"An eye for an eye will make the whole world blind."

Here's a timeline of Mahatma Gandhi's life -

  • October 2 1869 - Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is Born in Gujarat, India
  • 1876 - Mohandas Gandhi Begins Primary School in Rajkot
  • 1881 - Mohandes Gandhi Enters High School in Rajkot
  • May 1883 - Mohandes Gandhi Marries Kasturbai Makhanji in an Arranged Child Marriage
  • 1884 - Mohandes Gandhi Enters Rebellious Adolescent Phase, Engaging in Activities He Will Soon Reject
  • 1885 - Mohandes Gandhi's Father Dies at 63
  • September 4 1888 - Mohandes Gandhi Travels to London to Train as a Barrister
  • June 12, 1891 - Mohandes Gandhi Returns to India from London After Passing the Bar
  • April 1893 - Mohandes Gandhi Travels to South Africa to Work Under a Year-Long Contract with Dada Abdulla & Co., an Indian Firm
  • May 1893 - Mahatma Gandhi is Ejected from a South African Train, Motivating Him to Fight for Indian Rights in the British Colony
  • August 12 1894 - Mohandes Gandhi Founds the Natal Indian Congress
  • January 10 1897 - Mohandes Gandhi is Nearly Lynched by White Settlers in Durban upon Returning from a Brief Trip to Fetch his Family in India
  • 1899 - Gandhi Organizes an Indian Ambulance Corps of 1100 Men During His Service in the Boer War
  • November 1904 - Gandhi Founds the Phoenix Settlement
  • 1906 - Asiatic Law Amendment Ordinance Requires Asians in Transvaal to Carry Fingerprinted Identification
  • 1906 - Gandhi Organizes Stretcher-Bearer Corps that Serves During the Zulu Uprising
  • September 11 1906 - Gandhi Introduces Non-Violent Protest Philosophy of Satyagraha
  • January 10 1908 - Gandhi is Arrested for the First Time, for Refusing to Carry an Obligatory ID Card in South Africa
  • May 30 1910 - Gandhi Establishes the Tolstoy Farm, which Serves as a Base of Operations for His Activities in South Africa
  • November 6 1913 - Gandhi Begins "Great March" to Gain Indian Rights in South Africa
  • June 1914 - Gandhi Suspends South African Struggle After Winning Passage of the Indian Relief Act
  • January 9 1915 - Gandhi Receives Hero's Welcome Upon Returning to India from South Africa
  • April 1917 - Gandhi Goes to Champaran to Investigate Conditions of Local Farmers
  • June 17 1917 - Gandhi Establishes Sabarmati Ashram
  • January 1918 - Gandhi Begins Advocating on Behalf of Farmers in Kheda Subject to Oppressive Taxation During a Famine
  • March 1919 - British Government Passes the Rowlatt Act in Colonial India, Indefinitely Extending the Use of 'Emergency Measures'
  • September 1920 - The Gandhi Era of the Indian Independence Movement Begins with the Non-Cooperation Movement
  • December 1921 - Gandhi is Given Exclusive Authority Over the Indian National Congress
  • February 1922 - Nationalist Mob Sets Fire to a Police Station in Chauri Chaura Incident, Prompting Mahatma Gandhi to Call Off the Non-Cooperation Movement
  • March 10 1922 - Gandhi is Arrested by the British Government on Charges of Inciting Violence at Chauri Chaura
  • February 1924 - Gandhi is Released from Prison After Serving Only Two Years of a Six-Year Sentence Following Surgery for Appendicitis
  • September 1924 - Gandhi Begins Twenty-One Day Fast in an Attempt to Reconcile Hindus and Muslims
  • September 1925 - Gandhi Founds the All-India Spinners' Association
  • January 26 1930 - Declaration of the Independence of India
  • May 12 1930 - Mahatma Gandhi Embarks on the Salt Satyagraha
  • April 6 1930 - Mahatma Gandhi Arrives at Dandi, Gujarat, Concluding the Salt March
  • March 1931 - Lord Irwin, Viceroy of India, Signs the Gandhi-Irwin Pact
  • September 1932 - Gandhi Begins Six-Day Fast to Protest Separate Elections for Untouchables
  • September 1934 - Gandhi Announces Retirement from Politics to Focus on Village Economics
  • March 1939 - Gandhi Announces Fast-Unto-Death in the Effort to Form a People's Council in Rajkot
  • August 8 1942 - The All India Congress Committee Launches the Quit India Movement
  • August 9 1942 - Gandhi is Arrested, Along with the Entire Congress Working Committee
  • February 12 1944 - Kasturba Gandhi Dies at Age 74
  • March 1946 - The British Cabinet Mission Arrives in India
  • September 1946 - Formation of the Interim Government of India
  • June 15 1947 - United Kingdom Passes the Indian Independence Act 1947
  • August 15 1947 - The Partition of India
  • January 12 1948 - Mahatma Gandhi Announces Fast to End Hindu/Muslim Violence in Delhi
  • January 30 1948 - Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is Assassinated by Nathuram Godse

"On Gandhi: Don't ever forget, that we were not lead by a saint with his head in clouds, but by a master tactician with his feet on the ground"

Shashi Tharoor, Indian MP
